DOC LUNGHI

Romagna Mia

Paolo Melandri-Pier Damiano Benghi

In May 2023, and again between September and October 2024, the Romagna region was struck by devastating floods that turned familiar landscapes into scenes of destruction. This documentary delves into the human and environmental drama behind these events, linking them to climate change, which is making extreme weather phenomena increasingly frequent and intense.

About My Mom

Soner Sert

Caner, who went to Germany to study at university, returns to Türkiye to attend the fifth anniversary commemoration of his mother's death, and is greeted at the airport by his father Muharrem, whom he has not seen for a long time. Caner, who is going to set off on a long journey to the countryside of Türkiye to attend the commemoration where all the relatives will gather and perform religious rituals with his father, whom he sees as responsible for his mother's death, enters into a fierce struggle with his father on this journey. In this journey, where the sociopolitical environment of the country forms the background, things will get even more complicated when Caner's mother gets involved between the father and son on a dark night.

DOC CORTI

Moving Mountains

Andrea Costa

Moussa, a young man from Guinea, finds a home on the farm of Rita, a farmer in South Tyrol. Through daily routines, faith and challenges, the film explores the meaning of belonging and the need for family. Distant in origin but close in their desires, Moussa and Rita search together for a new balance.

DOC CORTI

A Little Fact

Polina Pribytkova, Maxim Pechersky

A jigsaw film assembled from Soviet archival footage from the 20s and 30s, preserving memories of the construction of a new and everyday life - the launch of the metro, hard work, cultural life and leisure. By combining two parallel stories - state film chronicles and diary entries - the film attempts to revise the historical archive and the usual perception of the time, bringing to the forefront the collective image of a hero from the crowd.

DOC CORTI

Riccardo I

Federico Cozzio

Through archival footage, the film explores the relationship between a man, Vittorio, and his beloved boat, Riccardo I. Set against the backdrop of profound changes affecting the Adriatic Sea and its shores in the 20th century, it captures the end of ancient seafaring traditions. As a child, Vittorio serves as a cabin boy on his father's boat; in his old age, he feels a deep urge to preserve the world of his childhood. With unwavering determination, he embarks on a mission to restore Riccardo I, the last sailing ship of its kind, dedicating himself to the project until his final breath.

“A Little Story of a Wall” investigates the impact of art upon people’s daily lives.
